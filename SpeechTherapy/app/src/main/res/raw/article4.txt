<p>
<span style="color:#ba6b6c;">Dysarthria</span> occurs when the muscles you use for speech are weak or you have difficulty controlling them.
Dysarthria often causes slurred or slow speech that can be difficult to understand.
</p>
</p>
Common causes of dysarthria include <span style="color:#ba6b6c;">nervous system disorders and conditions that cause facial paralysis</span> or tongue or throat muscle weakness.
<span style="color:#ba6b6c;">Certain medications</span> also can cause dysarthria.
Treating the underlying cause of your dysarthria may improve your speech. You may also need speech therapy.
For dysarthria caused by prescription medications, changing or discontinuing the medications may help.
</p>
<h3>Symptoms</h3>
<ul>
<li>Slurred speech</li>
<li>Slow speech</li>
<li>Inability to speak louder than a whisper or speaking too loudly</li>
<li>Rapid speech that is difficult to understand</li>
<li>Nasal, raspy or strained voice</li>
<li>Uneven or abnormal speech rhythm</li>
<li>Uneven speech volume</li>
<li>Monotone speech</li>
<li>Difficulty moving your tongue or facial muscles</li>
</ul>
<h3>When to see a doctor</h3>
<span style="color:#ba6b6c;">Dysarthria can be a sign of a serious condition.</span> See your doctor if you have sudden or unexplained changes in your
ability to speak.

<h3>Causes</h3>
In dysarthria, you may have <span style="color:#ba6b6c;">difficulty moving the muscles in your mouth, face or upper respiratory system</span> that control
speech. Conditions that may lead to dysarthria include:

<ul>
<li>Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS, or Lou Gehrig's disease)</li>
<li>Brain injury</li>
<li>Brain tumor</li>
<li>Cerebral palsy</li>
<li>Guillain-Barre syndrome</li>
<li>Head injury</li>
<li>Huntington's disease</li>
<li>Lyme disease</li>
<li>Multiple sclerosis</li>
<li>Muscular dystrophy</li>
<li>Myasthenia gravis</li>
<li>Parkinson's disease</li>
<li>Stroke</li>
<li>Wilson's disease</li>
<li>Some medications, such as certain sedatives and seizure drugs, also can cause dysarthria.</li>
</ul>
<h3>Complications</h3>
Because of the communication problems dysarthria causes, complications can include:

<span style="color:#ba6b6c;">Social difficulty.</span> Communication problems may affect your relationships with family and friends and make social
situations challenging.
<span style="color:#ba6b6c;">Depression.</span> In some people, dysarthria may lead to social isolation and depression.
