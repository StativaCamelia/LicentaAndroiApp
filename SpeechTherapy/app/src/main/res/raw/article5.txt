<h3>How are speech disorders diagnosed?</h3>
Many tests are available to diagnose speech disorders.

<h3>Denver articulation screening exam</h3>
<p>
The Denver articulation screening examination (DASE) is a commonly used testing system to diagnose articulation disorders.
This test <span style="color:#ba6b6c;">evaluates the clarity in pronunciation</span> in children between the ages of 2 and 7.
This five-minute test uses various exercises to assess the child’s speech.
</p>

<h3>Early language milestones scale 2</h3>
<p>
This test, created by neurodevelopmental pediatrician James Coplan, <span style="color:#ba6b6c;">determines a child’s language development.</span>
This test can quickly identify delayed speech or language disorders.
</p>
<h3>Peabody picture vocabulary test, revised</h3>
<p>
This test <span style="color:#ba6b6c;">measures a person’s vocabulary and ability to speak.</span>
The person will listen to various words and choose pictures that describe the words.
People who have severe intellectual disabilities and those who are blind won’t able to take this assessment.
The Peabody picture vocabulary test has been revised many times since its first version was administered in 1959.
</p>
